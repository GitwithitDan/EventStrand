Last updated: March 12, 2026  ·  Effective: March 12, 2026
This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, which ones Eventstrand uses, why we use them, and how you can control them. By using Eventstrand, you agree to our use of cookies as described here. This policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.
1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device by websites you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to site owners. Cookies may be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (remaining on your device until they expire or you delete them).

In addition to traditional cookies, we may use similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and pixel tags. This policy covers all such technologies collectively.

2. Who Sets Cookies on Eventstrand?

First-party cookies are set directly by Eventstrand (eventstrand.com). Third-party cookies may be set by services we use, such as analytics providers. We do not currently use advertising or tracking cookies from third-party ad networks.

4. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for Eventstrand to function and cannot be switched off. They are set in response to actions you take, such as logging in or filling out a form. They do not store personally identifiable information beyond what is required to deliver the service securely.

You cannot opt out of strictly necessary cookies. If you disable them via your browser settings, parts of the site will not work correctly.
5. Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with Eventstrand — which features are used most, where users encounter difficulties, and how performance can be improved. Data collected through analytics cookies is aggregated and anonymised; it is never sold or shared with third parties for commercial purposes.

You can opt out of analytics cookies at any time using our Consent Preferences panel below, or via your browser settings.

6. Preference Cookies

Preference cookies allow Eventstrand to remember information that changes the way the site looks or behaves. For example, your language preference or your saved calendar layout. Without these cookies, some personalisation features will reset each visit.

Because there is currently no universally accepted standard for how services should respond to DNT signals, Eventstrand does not currently alter its data collection in response to DNT signals. We will continue to monitor developments in this area.
